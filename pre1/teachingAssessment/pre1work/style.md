# Slide Style Guide — Pre1 Presentation

**Purpose:** Consistent, accessible design for the 9-slide HTML presentation (Oral Assessment 1).  
**Audience:** Non-specialist; must be readable and clear for everyone.

---

## Colour scheme

- **Primary:** One main theme colour (e.g. navy `#1a365d` or deep blue) — titles, key headings, accents.
- **Secondary:** One supporting colour (e.g. teal `#0d9488` or steel blue) — bullets, links, secondary emphasis.
- **Neutrals:** Background off-white or light grey (e.g. `#f8fafc` or `#f1f5f9`); body text dark (e.g. `#1e293b` or `#0f172a`).
- **Contrast:** Ensure minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio (WCAG) between text and background. Prefer **dark text on light background**.
- **Avoid:** Red–green only for meaning; do not rely on colour alone (use icons or labels too).

---

## Typography

- **Font family:** Sans-serif for screens (e.g. **system-ui**, **Segoe UI**, **Helvetica Neue**, or **Open Sans**). One font for headings, one for body (or same family with different weights).
- **Hierarchy:**
  - **Title slide / slide titles:** 44–60pt (2.75–3.75rem), bold.
  - **Subheadings:** 28–36pt (1.75–2.25rem), semibold.
  - **Body / bullets:** 24–28pt (1.5–1.75rem), regular. **Minimum 24pt for body.**
  - **Captions / small text:** 18–20pt if needed.
- **Line height:** 1.2–1.5 for body.
- **Rules:** Prefer bold for headings and key terms; avoid ALL CAPS for long blocks; use italics sparingly.

---

## Layout and spacing

- **One main idea per slide.** No more than ~6 lines of bullets per slide; ~6 words per line when possible.
- **White space:** 40–50% of slide can be empty; consistent margins and padding (e.g. 2rem or 3rem).
- **Alignment:** Left-align body and bullets; centre only for title slide or single-statement slides.
- **Consistency:** Same title position, same bullet style, same spacing across all 9 slides.

---

## Visual elements

- **Charts/tables:** One main chart per slide (e.g. slide 4: three groups, frequency & accuracy). Clear labels; minimal text; distinct colours per group (not red vs green only).
- **Icons:** Simple, consistent style (e.g. one icon set). Use for “guided,” “unguided,” “no AI” if helpful — no clip art.
- **Images:** Only if they support the message; high quality; consistent style.
- **Quotes:** One short quote allowed (e.g. significance slide); large enough to read (e.g. 22–26pt).

---

## Animation and transitions

- **Transitions:** Subtle (e.g. fade or none); same for all slides; quick (0.3–0.5s).
- **Animations:** Minimal; only if they help (e.g. bullets appear one by one). No distracting motion or sound.

---

## Accessibility checklist

- [ ] Text at least 24pt for body.
- [ ] Contrast ≥ 4.5:1 (text vs background).
- [ ] No information conveyed by colour alone (use text or icons too).
- [ ] Simple, clear language on slides; technical terms explained in one phrase or in speaker notes.
- [ ] Logical reading order; one main point per slide.
